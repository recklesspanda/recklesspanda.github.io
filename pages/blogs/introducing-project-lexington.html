Introducing Project Lexington

October 2, 2017

This is a preview of something I’ve been working on called Project Lexington, and I’m excited to share a sneak peak with you today!

The city of Lexington has the chance to reinvent and really put itself on the map. There is potential for Lexington to become a major hub for startups, innovation, and many other industries that either can’t or don’t want to shell out the cash required to headquarter in a big city. But, that growth could be stunted by not having a properly utilized downtown.

The idea for Project Lexington came about as a personal endeavor because I’ve found I like thinking about city planning, and as my first blog says, I’ve decided to take on this adventure of sharing more of my thoughts with you, whoever you are. I don’t really know what Project Lexington will be when it’s done, but it’ll be a collection of papers, drawings, blueprints, or any other media that helps convey my ideas about what to do with downtown. There are a couple main pillars I’d like to address over the course of this project, and as of now they are:

  • Housing

    • I want to modernize housing downtown, and make it truly affordable to young professionals in the 23-30 year old range.

  • Transportation

    • We have to stop thinking of public transportation as a second class citizen mode of getting around. Some things I hope to talk about are bus routes, separated pedestrian/cyclist highways, electric trams, and more creative parking structures.

  • Economics

    • A downtown cannot function if there’s no business going on.

  • Environment

    • There’s an opportunity to showcase new green technology in Lexington, especially since there are thousands of acres of farmland so close to Lexington that need to be protected for economic interests and tourism reasons.


However, the beauty of Project Lexington is that it won’t be just my ideas. I want to involve as many people as I can with the process, and get tons of ideas compiled together. I want the simple, the crazy, and everything in between. The community needs to be involved because that’s the essence of what a successful city is…a community.

I don’t care if you’re from Lexington or not, if you’ve ever had an idea for an urban city please take a second to share your idea with me! My contact info is below, and I’ve love to begin to connect and build rapport with those that read my blogs.
