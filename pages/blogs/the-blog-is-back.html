The Blog is Back

February 21, 2018

Sorry for being gone for so long...

Life caught up with me, and I haven't had much of a chance to get my blog started back up again.  But excuses are nothing but words, so enough of those.

At the beginning of 2018 I laid out some goals for myself under three main pillars: family, health, and wealth. I also got myself a white board to write all those goals down on and I've found it to help tremendously.  I doubt many of you really care about my personal goals, but I wanted to let you know what this newsletter had in store for this year.

Fashion
Within the past few months I've found myself really getting into men's and women's fashion, specifically streetwear.  Having only basic tailoring skills and a sewing machine, I've begun a project of modifying existing clothes I have and making new ones.  Right now the only piece I have made public is a pair of denim you can see on my website, but there's a few pieces in the works.

Design
Another thing I've gotten involved with recently is interior design.  Something I find especially cool is figuring out how to maximize small spaces like studios or odd size rooms for functionality while remaining pleasing to look at.  I'd describe my overall style as modern-contemporary with a fairly monochromatic color pallet.  If you need a visual of how I feel about interior design watch this video about Joe Sadler's house in LA.  No idea why, but I think this guy is the coolest, and his house is awesome.

Along with interior design I've also become very interested in urban design, and urban planning.  As you can tell, there hasn't been much headway with Project Lexington in a while, but that's because I've been struggling with how to best share my feelings and ideas about the city of Lexington with others.  I'm currently working on recreating the immediate downtown landscape in auto CAD, so I think when that's done I'll be able to better convey what I'm thinking.

Programming
I suppose with the right brain stuff above you have to include some left brain as well.  My code project for the year, unoriginally named JARVIS, is going to be an all encompassing home automation and virtual assistant platform.  I haven't yet made the "brain" of JARVIS, but I have been working on some widgets and smaller utility tools.  In the future I'll be making the Github repo public if you want to keep up with the progress of the project as well.

So there's a quick synopsis of what's in store for this year.  From now on I will be putting out a new post every Sunday evening, so be looking forward to those.  As always, if you want to know more about something I'm doing or just want to talk about an idea you have, feel free to get in contact with me.

--

Stephen
